Draft 1

Original Draft: Multimodal Literacy Narrative

Malcolm Dupuis · Professor Benjelloun · ENC 1101 · May 24, 2026

My literacy journey began, like anyone else, as soon as I was born and began interpreting the world around me for the first time. From my first words to the present day, my literacy has been and will continue to evolve and shape the way I think. My parents, naturally, were my first literary sponsors as they assisted my growth into the person I am today. Deborah Brandt defines a sponsor as someone who lends “their resources or credibility to the sponsored but also stand to gain benefits from their success” (Brandt, p. 167). My writing identity has developed through early parental guidance, extracurricular involvement, media exposure, and experiences within the school system.

My parents began reading to me when I was very young, and I largely attribute my proficiency in reading and writing to this. Their influence helped me develop vocabulary, reading ability, values, and professional communication. This eventually led me to placement in the gifted program, where I built writing, research, problem solving, teamwork, and relationship building skills.

After elementary school, I went to a full-time gifted program at Dunedin Middle School where I saw immense growth in literacy. My 6th grade ELA course, word stem practice, AP Research, and source-based writing helped me improve vocabulary, text analysis, research, and experimental writing.

Outside of school, music, hockey, Best Buddies, summer camp counseling, and Publix helped me become a better communicator. Hockey especially taught me to communicate differently with peers, coaches, and adults, connecting to Vershawn Ashanti Young’s discussion of code switching.

At Publix, I developed professional communication by learning to interact with customers, superiors, and coworkers. These experiences expanded my understanding of literacy beyond reading and writing into communication, tone, audience awareness, and professionalism.

Now that I am in college, I believe my experiences both in and outside school have helped prepare me for this class and my future. Kara Taczak explains that transfer matters in first-year writing because it teaches students to become more effective writers for the future (Taczak, p.303).

Works Cited

  • Brandt, Deborah. “Sponsors of Literacy.” College Composition and Communication, vol. 49, no. 2, 1998, pp. 165–185.
  • Taczak, Kara. “The Importance of Transfer in Your First Year Writing Course.” Writing Spaces: Readings on Writing, vol. 4, edited by Dana Driscoll et al., Parlor Press, 2020, pp. 301–315.
  • Young, Vershawn Ashanti. "'Nah, We Straight': An Argument Against Code Switching." JAC, vol. 29, no. 1/2, 2009, pp. 49–76.

New Draft

Revised Draft: Multimodal Literacy Narrative

Malcolm Dupuis · Professor Benjelloun · ENC 1101 · May 24, 2026

My literacy journey began, like anyone else as soon as I was born and began interpreting the world around me for the first time. From my first words to the present day, my literacy has been and will continue to evolve and shape the way I think. My parents, naturally, were my first literary sponsors as they assisted my growth into the person I am today. Deborah Brandt defines a sponsor as someone who lends “their resources or credibility to the sponsored but also stand to gain benefits from their success” (Brandt, p. 167).

My parents began reading to me when I was incredibly young, and I largely attribute my proficiency in reading and writing to this. The revised draft directly connects the image of me with my first book to early literacy development, vocabulary, and reading comprehension. It also connects my gifted creativity Olympics photo to interpersonal literacy, teamwork, collaboration, and problem solving.

In middle school and high school, my literacy grew through my 6th grade ELA course, word stem practice, standardized testing preparation, and AP Research. These experiences helped me develop a wider vocabulary, stronger writing ability, better source gathering, and a clearer understanding of the difference between literature review and original research.

Hockey became one of the largest influences on my communication and literacy. The revised draft connects the hockey image to identity, friendship, communication with coaches and peers, and code switching. This section links my experience to Vershawn Ashanti Young’s discussion of how people shift language depending on audience and setting.

Best Buddies and summer camp counseling helped me build interpersonal literacy through patience, tone, body language, and adapting communication for different audiences. The revised draft makes these connections clearer by explaining how these experiences taught me to communicate with people who process information differently.

Publix helped me develop professional literacy. The revised draft explains how I learned to read conversations, facial expressions, urgency, audience needs, professional tone, and concise workplace writing. These additions connect the job experience more directly to literacy and code switching.

Overall, the revised draft presents literacy as more than reading and writing. It includes verbal communication, body language, audience awareness, professional communication, and transfer. Kara Taczak’s idea of transfer supports the essay’s conclusion that literacy skills prepare me for future writing and communication situations.

Revision Memo

Multimodal Literacy Narrative: Revision Memo

Malcolm Dupuis · Professor Benjelloun · ENC 1101 · May 31, 2026

Although my multimodal literacy narrative first draft was mostly complete, after review I found that there were still some areas that were lacking. I felt that the overall structure and main content were sound. I used strong personal examples to demonstrate experiences with literacy through school, family, extracurriculars, and working. I used evidence from our text-based readings to support ideas such as my parents being literary sponsors as well as the connection between Vershawn Ashanti Young’s discussion on code switching and my experiences with hockey. These sections made clear connections between course readings and my own literary experiences. However, there were still some areas which needed development in the essay.

I found that many of the examples present were strong, but there was not a present tie back to the main idea of literacy. This made the narrative lack some direction as it focused more on overall personal growth rather than specifically literary growth. For instance, the sections on Publix and Best Buddies focused heavily on communication but did not discuss how those experiences related to literacy itself. I went back and edited it to explain how these experiences built literacy skills such as reading conversation cues, tone, and body language. This further promoted literacy is more than just reading and writing within my narrative, pushing me to consider ideas such as interpersonal literacy, professional literacy, and adapting communication styles.

The most significant feedback I received on my narrative was that the multimodal component of my essay was lacking as the photos were not directly tied to the text. This allowed me to realize that I had included photos which were personally meaningful but did not explain their purpose within the essay. In response, I made sure the images were more intentionally integrated into the essay, explaining their meaning and how it ties to the main idea of literacy. I referenced what the image showed and connected it to the literary concepts being discussed. For instance, I clarified how certain images represented literacy development, communication, or identity formation. Rather than just inserting an image for decoration, I tried to make sure they increased the overall effectiveness of the essay.

Overall, I agreed with most of the feedback I received as it involved improving clarity and giving more meaning to the multimodal elements. The revised drafts more directly explain the purpose of images used and ties them into the written argument. In addition, deeper literary analysis was done on topics such as interpersonal communication and code switching, as opposed to just referencing these ideas. However, I intentionally kept the narrative feeling more personal and reflective as opposed to academic. Moving forward, I want to refine transitions between ideas and further polish sentence structure in order to improve the flow of the essay. Overall, the revision process substantially strengthened my essay and gave me a deeper understanding of multimodality and literacy.
